# Content Cadence Calibration

## Suggested Initial Rhythm

This is a **suggested starting cadence**, not a contract. Adjust as you find your rhythm. The goal is sustainable publishing that compounds over years, not sprint-to-burnout.

### Blog Posts (Long-form Essays)
**Cadence:** One essay every two weeks (fortnightly)  
**Rationale:**
- Sustainable for a multi-hyphenate (engineering + music + coaching + writing)
- Frequent enough to build momentum
- Infrequent enough to allow depth per piece

**Minimum viable post:**
- 800-1500 words
- One clear idea, developed
- Links to at least one other register (sovereignty throughline)

**Topics seed list:**
- Sovereignty (macro finance, Bitcoin, exit strategies)
- Systems thinking (engineering patterns applied to creative work)
- Inner work (Vipassana, men's circles, coaching insights)
- Music + culture (artist management, DJ practice, flow arts)
- Integration (how the four registers inform each other)

### /now Updates
**Cadence:** Once per month  
**Rationale:**
- Low-friction snapshot of current state across all registers
- Signals life/activity without requiring essay-length depth
- Readers checking in see what's alive

**Update triggers:**
- Last Sunday of each month (pairs with analytics review)
- Or when a major shift happens (new project launched, retreat facilitated, release managed, etc.)

### Library Entries
**Cadence:** One book added per essay (opportunistic, not scheduled)  
**Rationale:**
- As you write essays, books surface as references
- Add them to `/library` when they're load-bearing in a piece
- Keeps the library organic, not performative

### Register Pages (/coaching, /dev, /music, /projects)
**Cadence:** Quarterly refresh (every 3 months)  
**Rationale:**
- Static content, not dynamic
- Update when projects ship or offerings change
- Example: new Echological artist signed → update `/music`

## Publishing Workflow

### Vault → Publish → Live
1. **Compose** in `Projects/Dyallo Blog/posts/[slug].md` (Obsidian vault)
2. **Review** — Is the sovereignty throughline visible? Does it link registers?
3. **Publish** — `bun run publish` (syncs vault → repo, commits, pushes)
4. **Verify** — Check `https://dyallo.se/blog/[slug]` renders correctly
5. **Announce** (optional) — Share on social if it feels aligned (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, personal channels)

### Draft → Live Timeline
- **Idea captured:** Voice note or Obsidian daily note
- **Outline drafted:** 1-2 hours
- **First draft:** 2-4 hours (uninterrupted writing block)
- **Edit pass:** 1 hour (next day, fresh eyes)
- **Publish:** `bun run publish` (instant)
- **Total:** One focused work session + one editing session = fortnightly cadence is realistic

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- ❌ **Perfectionism paralysis** — Ship the essay when it's load-bearing, not when it's perfect
- ❌ **Comparison anxiety** — This is not a media brand competing for virality; it's a convergence node compounding slowly
- ❌ **Topic drift** — If an essay doesn't touch at least one of the four registers, it may belong elsewhere (or needs reframing)
- ❌ **Forcing cadence** — If life demands focus elsewhere (retreat facilitation, album release, major engineering push), pause publishing without guilt. The site compounds over years, not weeks.

## Calibration Checkpoints

### After 3 months (August 2026)
- Review: Is fortnightly sustainable?
- Adjust: Weekly? Monthly? Stay at fortnightly?
- Evidence: Analytics (which topics landed), personal energy (is this generative or draining?)

### After 6 months (November 2026)
- Review: What themes emerged organically?
- Adjust: Double down on resonant topics, retire forced themes
- Evidence: Top 5 posts by traffic, reader feedback (email replies, social mentions)

### After 12 months (May 2027)
- Review: Is the blog serving the sovereignty stack? Is it feeding the other registers (consulting leads, coaching inquiries, artist opportunities)?
- Adjust: If yes, continue. If no, diagnose why and pivot.

## Success Metrics (Non-Vanity)

The blog is succeeding if:
- **You're writing what you'd write anyway** (it's clarifying your own thinking, not performing for an audience)
- **One reader per month emails** with a substantive question or insight (depth over breadth)
- **The four registers feel more integrated** (essays surface connections you hadn't seen)
- **The site feeds other work** (consulting inquiry, coaching lead, artist management connection traced back to a post)

Vanity metrics (pageviews, subscriber count, social shares) are lagging indicators. The leading indicator is: **Are you still excited to publish the next piece?**

If that's a yes, the cadence is working.
